HOKKAIDO

Hokkaido Panoramic Flower Gardens, also known as Shikisai-no-oka, are a breathtaking sight to behold. Spanning across a sprawling 15-hectare ground, the gardens are home to over 30 kinds of flowers, including tulips, oriental poppies, lavenders, sunflowers, petunias, and many more. The flowers bloom seasonally from April to October, creating a vibrant and ever-changing display of colors.

Itsukushima Shinto Shrine

Itsukushima Shinto Shrine, located on the island of Itsukushima (popularly known as Miyajima), is a marvel of spiritual architecture and natural beauty1. Best known for its “floating” torii gate, the shrine is a harmonious blend of human creativity and nature. The shrine complex, dating back to the 12th century, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a national treasure of Japan.

Osaka

Osaka Tower, also known as Tsūtenkaku, is a well-known landmark of Osaka, Japan1. The name Tsūtenkaku translates to "Tower Reaching Heaven". It stands at a height of 103 meters, with the main observation deck located at a height of 91 meters.

MOUNT FUJI

Mount Fuji, standing at 3,776 meters, is Japan’s highest mountain and a symbol of the country1. It is renowned for its perfectly symmetrical cone shape, covered in snow for several months of the year. The mountain has been a sacred site for centuries and has inspired countless works of art, literature, and music. Lake Kawaguchiko, also known as Kawaguchiko, is one of the Fuji Five Lakes and is the most easily accessible with direct bus and train connections to Tokyo. The lake offers some of the best views of Mount Fuji, particularly from its northern shores. These views are especially breathtaking during the cherry blossom season around mid-April and the autumn colors around the first half of November

KYOTO

Kyoto, officially known as Kyoto City, is the capital city of Kyoto Prefecture in the Kansai region of Japan’s largest and most populous island of Honshu.Over the centuries, Kyoto was destroyed by many wars and fires, but due to its exceptional historic value, the city was dropped from the list of target cities for the atomic bomb and escaped destruction.
